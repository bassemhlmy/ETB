Egypt Travel Visa Guide

Planning your trip to Egypt starts with one essential step: securing your entry visa. Requirements vary by nationality, but this guide covers everything you need to know — from visa-free entry to visa on arrival and online applications.

Quick Overview

  • Visa types available for Egypt
  • Visa-free countries
  • Visa on arrival eligibility
  • How to apply for an e-Visa

Visa Types for Egypt

Travelers can enter Egypt using one of the following visa options:

  • Visa-Free Entry – Available for select nationalities
  • Visa on Arrival – Issued at Egyptian airports
  • e-Visa – Apply online before travel

Visa-Free Entry

Citizens of certain countries can enter Egypt without a visa for up to 90 days.

Bahrain Hong Kong Oman Macao
Kuwait Saudi Arabia UAE Malaysia

Special Entry Conditions

Some nationalities may qualify for visa-free entry under specific conditions:

Country Condition
Jordan Valid passport (5+ years) with no restrictions
Lebanon / Morocco / Tunisia Age restrictions apply (under 16 or above 50)
Sudan / South Sudan Age or gender-based eligibility
China Hotel booking, return ticket, or proof of funds required

Visa on Arrival

Many travelers can obtain a visa upon arrival at Egyptian airports, valid for up to 30 days.

  • Passport valid for at least 6 months
  • Visa fee: $25 USD (cash only)
  • Available for EU, UK, USA, Canada, Australia, and more

Main Entry Airports

  • Cairo International Airport
  • Sphinx International Airport (Giza)
  • Borg El Arab Airport (Alexandria)
  • Sharm El Sheikh Airport
  • Hurghada Airport
  • Luxor Airport

Apply for an Egypt e-Visa

The easiest way to get your visa is online. The Egypt e-Visa allows:

  • Single-entry: Stay up to 30 days (valid for 6 months)
  • Multiple-entry: Multiple visits within 3 months

How to Apply

  • Valid passport (minimum 6–8 months)
  • Complete the online application form
  • Pay using credit/debit card
  • Upload passport copy
  • Receive visa within ~5 business days
